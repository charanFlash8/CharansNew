The Big Problem

Every day in India, thousands of kilos of edible food are wasted by hotels, weddings, hostels, and homes—while millions go to bed hungry, including children, elderly, and migrants. Most food donation efforts are unorganized, urban-centric, and not real-time.

Our Solution

BhojanRight leverages data, community networks, and legal frameworks to treat food access as a right, not a charity. We prioritize invisible communities like street children, migrant workers, and slums.

How It Works

  • App + Website: Food donors report surplus instantly. Volunteers and mapped beneficiaries are alerted in real-time via SMS or WhatsApp.
  • Hunger Ambassadors: Our trained volunteers and workers map hunger hotspots, report food needs, and build local compassion ecosystems.
  • Legal & Policy Advocacy: We fight for a "Right to Surplus Food," food waste laws, tax benefits, and public logistics support.
  • BhojanBoxes: Smart, monitored community food fridges in every district, managed by local governments and NGOs.

This model ensures food safety, dignity, and accountability while transforming food waste into a rights-based solution.
